Questions before starting a website

Do I need to arrive with everything figured out? +

No. A short description of your business and what the website should help with is enough to start. If you already have text, images, logos, or examples you like, they help; if not, we can shape the first direction together.

What happens first? +

I send three initial website ideas so you can quickly see which direction feels right.

How do we shape the website? +

We decide the style, pages, and features together, then estimate the cost based on the complexity needed.

Can I see more about how the website is crafted? +

Yes. If you want a deeper look at the thinking behind the work, the Approach page walks through how I shape the first direction, choose what the page needs to prove, and refine the site around real screens. Read the Approach page.

What happens after the direction is agreed? +

I build and deliver the website. Simple template-like one-page websites are usually ready in 3 days after you provide the content and direction, subject to scope.

Are domain and hosting included? +

Domain and hosting are separate costs. I can help you understand and set them up, but they are separate from the website build itself.

Can one of the showcases be used as a starting template? +

Yes. The showcases are examples rather than fixed packages; a direction you like can be shaped around your real business, branding, wording, images, services, and goals.

Can the website be improved after delivery? +

Yes. Follow-up edits and improvements can be scoped after delivery, so the site can keep improving over time.

How do I contact you about a website? +

Use the dedicated contact page. A short message about your business, your preferred style, and what the website needs to help with is enough to start. Open the contact page.
